Vision 2050 City Documents

Official reports, plans, and council materials from the City of Berkeley

What is Vision 2050? In 2018, Berkeley voters approved Measure R, advising the Mayor to engage citizens and experts in developing a 30-year plan for climate-smart, technologically-advanced, equitable infrastructure. The initiative produced a series of official reports, community engagement processes, and policy actions — culminating in Measure FF (the SAFE STREETS initiative) and the 2025 Realize Vision 2050 Task Force update.

Founding & Framework

Core Vision 2050 Reports

May 2020 · Vision 2050 Task Force

Sustainable and Resilient Infrastructure: Creating a Better Future for Berkeley

The foundational Vision 2050 Framework report. Prepared by a 40+ member resident task force, it lays out core values (equity, resilience, sustainability), surveys Berkeley's infrastructure systems, and recommends five strategies for a 30-year modernization plan. Adopted by City Council in September 2020.

April 2022 · City of Berkeley

Strategic Asset Management Plan

Comprehensive assessment of Berkeley's infrastructure assets — streets, sewers, stormwater, buildings, parks, fleet — with a "cradle to grave" management approach. A key implementation step from the Vision 2050 recommendations, establishing how the City tracks and prioritizes infrastructure lifecycle maintenance.

December 2, 2025 · City Council Special Meeting

Realize Vision 2050: An Update to Vision 2050 and Recommendations to Address Berkeley's Infrastructure

Final report of Mayor Ishii's Realize Vision 2050 Task Force, submitted October 23, 2025. Reviews the five years since the original framework, assesses progress (including passage of Measure FF), addresses accelerating climate threats (wildfire, sea level rise), rising construction costs, and national funding uncertainty. Recommends integrated planning, new technologies, improved project delivery, and a future infrastructure bond measure for November 2026.
